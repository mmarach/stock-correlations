About Diary Dash

Diary Dash is a no-nonsense events planning and management portal.

Adding New Events

Diary Dash allows you to add events to your diary and keep track of them, easily knowing what the next upcoming events are! To add a new event, simply click on Add a New Event in Manage My Diary panel, specify the event details, and click on Add.

Viewing and Managing Upcoming Events

To view all of your upcoming events, click on Upcoming Events. For each event you will see a small indicator showing in how many days the event is due. If the event is due in less than a week, the indicator will appear yellow. Otherwise, the indicator will appear grey.

To change the event’s details, or to delete it altogether, click on the event’s title. If you want to view all upcoming events of the same type as the event of interest to you, simply click on the event’s Type.

Viewing and Managing Past Events

To view your past events, click on Past Events. Diary Dash allows you to rate your past events, so that you can easily know which type of events you enjoy the most and plan accordingly! To rate a past event, click on its title and click on Rate. You can then assign the event a rating between one and five.

For past events which have been rated already, you will see a small indicator showing their rating. The indicator’s colour will depend on the event’s rating so that you can easily tell whether you enjoyed a given event!

Getting Activity Suggestions

Don’t worry if you don’t have any ideas for any fun activities, as Diary Dash can help you find things to do! To get an activity suggestion based on criteria specified by you, click on Suggest an Activity in Manage My Diary panel.

If you’d like a completely random suggestion, check out I’m Feeling Lucky! and see what you get!

Bored API

Diary Dash utilises Bored API to generate activity suggestions. Every generated activity has an accessibility factor and a price factor assigned to it.

Accessibility factor describes how simple or possible an event is to do, with zero being the most and one being the least accessible. Price factor describes the potential monetary cost the activity involves, with zero being free and one being very expensive.

For more information on Bored API, see their official documentation.
